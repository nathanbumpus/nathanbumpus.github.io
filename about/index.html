About Me

I recently finished my Masters Degree in Bioinformatics at the University of Maine in May of 2019. During my time in graduate school I aspired to gain the computer science skills that would make me an ideal candidate for the role of an entry level bioinformatics analyst or research assistant. I have completed data analysis projects in Python using using scipy, matplotlib, pandas and numpy. I have used various R packages such as edgeR, DESeq2 and Pathview to explore and analyze large genomic datasets. For my graduate research project I developed a pipeline facilitating the use of Trinity software in producing de novo transcriptome assemblies on the high performance computing clusters at the University of Maine. Recently as my own side project I wrote a python program that allows me to store literary works from open source sites like Project Gutenberg in large strands of DNA. Not very useful but kind of fun.

Before I was a graduate student I worked as a Medical Laboratory Technologist for about seven years in various hospital settings. The most notable was a level one trauma center in Portland, Maine. There I worked on the evening shift in every department of a stat laboratory. I was also responsible for any coagulation factor assays that might arise during that time.

During my undergraduate career at the University of Maine at Augusta I excelled as a Medical Laboratory Technology and Biology student and was awarded the Celeste Cote Memorial Scholarship for the top student in my curriculum. I also took courses in Molecular Cell Biology, Genetics and Biochemistry. I graduated suma cum laude with a GPA of around 3.9 with an Associates of Science in Medical Laboratory Technology and a Bachelor of Arts in Biology.

In my free time I enjoy backpacking, camping, volunteering at a local food bank, making paper airplanes and daisy chaining emulators through virtual machines to play old video games.
